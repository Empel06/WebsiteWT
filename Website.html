The Project

For our project, we are creating a website to display the CPU temperature in real-time on a graph. This site will provide continuous monitoring and visualization of the CPU's thermal performance. Once completed, the website will be deployed online on a server, making it accessible for remote monitoring and analysis.

CPU

A CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the main component of a computer responsible for interpreting and executing most of the commands from the computer's hardware and software. It performs basic arithmetic, logic, control, and input/output operations specified by the instructions in the program. Often referred to as the "brain" of the computer, it significantly affects the system's overall performance. Modern CPUs contain multiple cores, which allow them to process multiple tasks simultaneously, enhancing multitasking capabilities and performance.

Temperature

A CPU generates heat during its operation due to the electrical power it consumes. When the CPU processes data, millions of transistors inside it switch on and off rapidly. This constant switching, along with resistance in the electrical circuits, causes energy to be dissipated as heat. The more intensive the computational tasks, the more power is consumed, leading to higher heat production. Efficient cooling systems, such as heat sinks, fans, and thermal paste, are essential to dissipate this heat and maintain optimal operating temperatures.

Graph

We are displaying the CPU temperature in real-time on a graph. This allows us to monitor the temperature continuously, ensuring that we can detect and respond to any potential overheating issues promptly. The graph updates dynamically, providing an up-to-date visual representation of the CPU's thermal performance.
